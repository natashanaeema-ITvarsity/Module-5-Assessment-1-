Mercury

The innermost planet of the solar system and the eighth in size and mass. Its closeness to the Sun and its smallness make it the most elusive of the planets visible to the unaided eye.

The difficulty in seeing it notwithstanding, Mercury was known at least by Sumerian times, some 5,000 years ago. In Classical Greece it was called Apollo when it appeared as a morning star just before sunrise and Hermes, the Greek equivalent of the Roman god Mercury, when it appeared as an evening star just after sunse

Mercury facts

  • Diameter: 4,879km
  • Mass: 3.29 x 10^23 kg(0.06 Earths)
  • Moons: none
  • Orbit distance: 57,909,227 km (0.39 AU)
  • Orbit period: 88 days
  • Surface temperature: -173° to 427°C
  • First record: 14th century BC
  • Recorded by: Assyrian astronomers
